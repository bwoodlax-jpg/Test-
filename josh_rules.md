## CORE TRADING RULES (The Foundational Framework)

### Rule 1 — There Are Rules. Learn Them.
> *"Whether trading NASDAQ, Blue-chip, or Penny stocks, OTC Pink Sheets — there are rules, and you better learn them."* *[direct]*

Different asset classes have completely different playbooks. You don't trade an OTC Pink Sheet the same way you trade Apple. Full stop.

### Rule 2 — This Is NOT a Get-Rich-Quick Scheme
- Repeated constantly, especially to new members
- Building real wealth takes time, discipline, consistency
- Anyone promising overnight riches is selling you something dangerous
- The goal is NET P&L over months and years — not one home run

### Rule 3 — Paper Trade First
- **Before real money hits the market, you simulate.** Period.
- Understand mechanics, practice entries and exits, build confidence — then scale up
- Losing fake money teaches you more than losing real money before you're ready

### Rule 4 — Stick to a Strict, Consistent Budget
- Every trade comes from a defined allocation — never blow past your budget
- Consistency in position sizing is how you survive long enough to win
- The budget protects your psychology as much as your account

### Rule 5 — Build a Long-Term Portfolio First
- Active trading is built on top of a foundation of quality long-term holds
- Blue chips and large-cap NASDAQ names anchor the portfolio
- Don't swing-trade everything — some positions are for holding, not flipping

### Rule 6 — Net P&L Is the Scoreboard
- One massive win doesn't make you profitable if 10 losses cancel it out
- Track everything. Know your real numbers.
- "One good trade doesn't make you a good trader."

### Rule 7 — Know the Difference Between a Catalyst and a Pump
- **Catalyst-driven move**: press release, earnings, FDA approval, macro news — legitimate reason for the move
- **Pump**: manufactured hype, social media run-up with no substance — high-risk, not a trade, a gamble
- If you can't identify the catalyst, you don't have a trade — you have a prayer

### Rule 8 — Know Your OTC/Pink Sheet Rules Cold
- OTC Pink Sheets are a different animal — **higher risk, more volatile, more manipulation**
- Rules: smaller position size, tighter stop discipline, only trade with a clear catalyst, know your exit before entry
- These aren't long-term holds — get in, get out, don't get greedy

### Rule 9 — Losses Are Lessons
- Every loss has something to teach — track what went wrong and why
- The community learns collectively; share the mistake so someone else doesn't repeat it
- Cutting a loss at 20% is discipline. Riding it to -80% is ego.

### Rule 10 — Not Trading IS a Valid Trade
- On slow days, in uncertain markets, the best position is sometimes no position
- Waiting for the right setup is a skill — don't force trades out of boredom or FOMO

## OPTIONS FRAMEWORK (Josh's Approach)

### ITM vs. OTM
- **In The Money (ITM)**: higher premium, lower risk, behaves more like owning shares
  - *Josh's general preference for directional plays* — more certainty, more intrinsic value
- **Out of The Money (OTM)**: cheaper, higher risk — only worth it if you're expecting a large, fast move
  - "OTM options can be profitable if you expect a big move, but they're riskier and less expensive." *[direct]*

### The Ghetto Spread — Josh's Signature Strategy
**How it works:**
1. Buy a call or put option on a strong catalyst play
2. Let it increase in value (the move is working in your favor)
3. Sell a further OTM call or put for **more than what you originally paid** for the first contract
4. Result: Your original position is now **free** — zero cost basis, with upside still open if the stock keeps moving
5. You've locked in profit without using a day trade

> *"The idea of a Ghetto Spread is securing profits on a Call or Put option without using a day trade, and giving yourself a chance to make a little more if the stock moves in your favor."* *[direct]*

**Famous trade:** Used the Ghetto Spread to turn ~$500 into $40,000–$50,000 on a single play.  
**Notable stat:** On one live stream, Josh executed a put option that returned 1,000% gains in 15 minutes.

### Options Basics Josh Drills
- Options value is tied to whether the asset is "in the money" or "out of the money"
- Intrinsic value vs. time value — understand what you're paying for
- Premium erodes — time decay (theta) works against you on OTM holds
- Only buy options on liquid tickers — wide spreads kill your edge on illiquid names

Strategy Methodology
Strategy type
Catalyst-Confirmed Technical Swing. Hybrid approach: a news catalyst (earnings, upgrade, launch, macro event) triggers the search for a trade; a technical setup confirms the entry. Neither signal alone is sufficient — both must align before the order is placed.
Does volume play into this strategy?
YES — REQUIRED
Volume ≥1.5× the 20-day ADV on the entry candle. Volume is the institutional participation signal. High volume + catalyst = real money moving = tradeable setup. Low volume + catalyst = retail noise — the move will likely fade. Skip thin entries.
Does SMA50 play into this strategy?
YES — TREND FILTER
Long entries only when price is above the 50-day SMA. If price is below the 50-day, the trend is against you — do not fight it on a weekly swing. Exception: price testing SMA50 with a live catalyst and volume surge is valid with a tighter stop. A declining SMA50 negates even that exception.
Holding period
2–4 trading days maximum. Enter Mon/Tue on confirmation; target hit or hard exit by Thursday close. Zero weekend holds on a swing position.
Technical Entry Filters
1. Catalyst trigger (primary signal)
Must identify a specific, verifiable catalyst before entry: earnings beat, product launch, analyst upgrade, macro data print. No catalyst = no trade. If you cannot name the catalyst precisely, you do not have a trade.
2. Volume confirmation (entry gate)
Entry candle must show ≥1.5× 20-day ADV. Green candle on 2× average volume = institutional accumulation. That is the wave you want to ride — not lead.
3. SMA50 position (trend gate)
Price above SMA50 = uptrend confirmed, take the long. Price at SMA50 with catalyst + volume = valid entry with tighter stop. Price below SMA50 = no new longs, no exceptions.
4. Risk/reward minimum (quality gate)
Minimum 2:1 R/R before the order button is touched. Preferred 3:1. If the stop is $1 below entry, the target must be ≥$2 above. Below 2:1 the math does not work over a sample of trades.
Rules & Guardrails
1 Size = exactly 1% of total bankroll. No exceptions, no adding. The full position is at risk from entry.
2 Stop set at entry — non-negotiable. Place it at the recent swing low or 2× the daily ATR below entry. It does not move after placement.
3 Minimum 2:1 R/R before the order is placed. Preferred 3:1. Calculate it before touching the button — not after.
4 Volume ≥1.5× the 20-day ADV on entry candle. If volume is thin on your entry bar, wait for the next candle or skip the trade entirely.
5 SMA50 trend filter applies to every new long. Price must be above the 50-day moving average. TAP (now archived) was the canonical Rule 5 violation — below SMA50, no new swing longs.
6 Name the catalyst before entry. Earnings beat, product launch, analyst upgrade, macro print. If you cannot name it specifically, you do not have a trade.
7 Exit by Thursday close. Weekly swings expire on a weekly clock. No carrying a swing thesis into the weekend without a hard, live catalyst still open.
8 No averaging down. One entry. One stop. One exit. If the stop is hit, the thesis was wrong. Accept the loss and move on.

SMA Stack Scanner — Bullish 200/150/100/50 Alignment
The Signal: When all three shorter SMAs (50, 100, 150-day) rise above the 200-day SMA simultaneously, the long-term trend has structurally shifted. This bullish SMA stack — Price > SMA(50) > SMA(100) > SMA(150) > SMA(200) — means the 200-day has crossed below all three, confirming the trend has flipped long-term. The 200 is now the floor, with each shorter SMA acting as a rising support layer above it.

Why it matters: Three simultaneous golden crosses (50>200, 100>200, 150>200) in the same stock is rare. Freshly-formed stacks (≤20 days) are the most actionable — you catch the structural shift early, before institutions fully position.

Q1
What is the CPT 2027 OB unbundling and why is it happening?
The AMA is replacing the 30+ year old global maternity care package with a service-based framework. Starting January 1, 2027, the single global code that bundled all prenatal visits, delivery, and postpartum care is eliminated. Each phase of maternity care — antepartum, labor management, delivery, and postpartum — is now reported with separate, individually billed codes.

The AMA's stated reasons include: patterns of care have become far more complex, team-based and shift-based care models are now the norm, increasing labor complexity and induction rates are not reflected in current coding, and the old model provided no data visibility into individual care phases. This change affects every OB/GYN practice billing under the current global model.
Q2
When does the new coding structure take effect?
Effective Date: January 1, 2027
All 17 deleted global and antepartum codes expire on December 31, 2026. Services rendered on or after January 1, 2027 must use the new code set. There is no parallel reporting period. Claims submitted for 2027 dates of service using deleted codes will be rejected.

Q4
How do I bill for antepartum office visits starting January 1, 2027?
Each antepartum visit is reported individually using standard Evaluation and Management (E/M) codes — the same codes used for regular office visits. The AMA guideline states that antepartum care is reported with the appropriate E/M code based on the location of the patient.
CodeUse For
99202–99205New patient office or outpatient visit — patient's first OB visit
99211–99215Established patient office or outpatient visit — all subsequent prenatal visits
99221–99223Initial hospital inpatient or observation — antepartum admission
99231–99233Subsequent hospital care — continued antepartum inpatient management
98000–98015Telehealth services — antepartum telemedicine encounters

E/M code selection rules apply — select by Medical Decision Making (MDM) or total time. Append Modifier TH to all antepartum E/M claims.
Q5
What is Modifier TH and when do I use it?
Modifier TH — AMA Confirmed
Modifier TH = "Obstetrical treatment/services, antepartum or postpartum." Apply Modifier TH to all antepartum and postpartum E/M claims. This identifies the visit as pregnancy-related, ensures correct payer processing, and supports maternity care analytics. Confirmed by AMA in the June 2, 2026 CPT Coding Primer Webinar.
Without Modifier TH, some payers may process these E/M visits as standard office visits and deny or underpay. Modifier TH does not apply to labor management codes (59080–59083) or delivery codes — it is specific to antepartum and postpartum E/M services.
Q6
How does Medical Decision Making (MDM) apply to prenatal visits?
MDM has three elements: Problems Addressed, Amount and Complexity of Data Reviewed, and Risk of Complications. The AMA confirms that pregnancy is evaluated under "Problems Addressed." The complexity of the pregnancy drives the MDM level.

Quick Reference — Prenatal Visit MDM Levels:
  • 99212–99213 (Straightforward/Low): Routine uncomplicated pregnancy, normal vitals, labs normal, no new concerns, minimal management.
  • 99214 (Moderate): Managed complication — well-controlled gestational hypertension or diet-controlled GDM. Data reviewed, monitoring plan adjusted.
  • 99215 (High): High-risk visit — poorly controlled GDM, IUGR concern, multiple management decisions, referral to MFM or specialist.
Critical note from AMA: documentation to support E/M code selection is key, particularly on Risk. Every prenatal note must independently support the level billed.
Q7
What are the new labor management codes and what do they include?
Four new labor management codes replace all labor-during-delivery bundled services. Reported per physician, per patient, per calendar date.
CodeDescriptor
59080Initial day labor management; straightforward, per day
59081Initial day labor management; complex, per day
59082Subsequent day labor management; straightforward, per day
59083Subsequent day labor management; complex, per day

Included in all labor management codes:
  • Interim physical examinations during labor
  • Collection and interpretation of physiologic data (partograms, tocometric data, vital signs, pulse oximetry)
  • Induction or augmentation of labor (mechanical cervical ripening, prostaglandins, oxytocin, amniotomy)
A delivery code may be reported on the same calendar date as a labor management code, even when the same physician provides both services.
Q8
How do I determine if labor management is "straightforward" or "complex"?
The AMA added a complexity determination table to the CPT 2027 guidelines. Straightforward labor requires ALL six criteria to be met simultaneously. If even one criterion is not met, the encounter is complex.
LevelCriteria
STRAIGHTFORWARDALL six must be met: (1) Singleton vertex presentation; (2) Routine maternal/fetal monitoring; (3) Fetal monitoring not requiring physician intervention; (4) Normal progression of labor or routine induction/augmentation; (5) Stable medical conditions not requiring additional management; (6) No previous cesarean delivery.
COMPLEXANY single deviation from the straightforward criteria. Examples include: multiple gestation, non-vertex presentation, fetal monitoring requiring intervention, abnormal labor progression, deteriorating condition, VBAC attempt (prior cesarean).
Q9
What if labor starts as straightforward but becomes complex during the same calendar day?
AMA Confirmed Rule — Same-Day Complexity Upgrade
If labor management begins as straightforward (59080) but transitions to complex on the same calendar date, report ONLY the complex code (59081) for that day. Do not report both straightforward and complex codes on the same date. One labor management code per physician per patient per calendar day.
Exception for care transfers: If the complexity increase results in a transfer of care from one group to a separate group (e.g., a CNM transfers to a physician of a different specialty group), each provider may report their own labor management code for that calendar date.
Q10
Does a planned or scheduled cesarean require a labor management code?
AMA Confirmed Rule — Planned Cesarean
A planned or scheduled cesarean has NO labor management code associated with it. Report only the cesarean delivery code. Labor management codes apply only when labor management services are actually performed.
What CAN be reported with a planned primary cesarean (no labor performed):
  • 59502 — Primary cesarean delivery
  • 99221–99223 — Initial hospital inpatient or observation E/M (may be separately reported on the same date when performed)
For a planned repeat cesarean (no labor), the admission/intake E/M is already included in the valuation of 59503 and cannot be separately reported on the same calendar date.

Q12
What happens to postpartum care coding?
All current postpartum CPT codes (including 59430 and the postpartum portions of global codes) are deleted January 1, 2027. Postpartum care is now reported using E/M codes:

Inpatient postpartum (facility birth):
  • 99231–99233 — Subsequent hospital care, reported each management day until discharge
  • Appropriate discharge day management code on the day of discharge
Outpatient postpartum after discharge:
  • 99212–99215 — Established patient office/outpatient E/M for each postpartum visit
Apply Modifier TH to all postpartum E/M claims. Routine postpartum care on the delivery date is included in the delivery code — do not bill it separately.
Q13
How do I handle patients whose prenatal care starts in 2026 and delivery is in 2027?
The AMA addressed this transition scenario directly. The rule is based on the date of service, not the delivery date:
  • Antepartum visits in 2026 → report using 2026 CPT rules: 59425 (4–6 visits), 59426 (7 or more visits), or individual E/M codes if fewer than 4 visits in 2026.
  • Antepartum visits in 2027 → report each visit individually with the appropriate E/M code under the new 2027 framework.

AMA planning guidance: pregnancies with first 10-week visits between January and May 2026 will likely deliver under the old global codes. Pregnancies beginning visits in September 2026 or later will have 3 or fewer prenatal visits in 2026 and require E/M reporting for those visits. Update your billing workflow and EHR charge capture before December 31, 2026.
Q14
What about VBAC patients — trial of labor after cesarean (TOLAC)?
A Trial of Labor After Cesarean (TOLAC) or VBAC attempt DOES involve labor management and labor management codes apply. However, VBAC attempts are complex by default because "no prior cesarean" is a required criterion for straightforward labor management.

TOLAC/VBAC Coding:
  • Labor management: 59080/59081 (initial day) or 59082/59083 (subsequent day) — complex (59081 or 59083) by default due to prior cesarean history
  • Successful vaginal delivery: 59432 — vaginal delivery after previous cesarean
  • Failed VBAC requiring cesarean: 59503 — repeat cesarean delivery
The delivery code is determined by the actual delivery outcome. Labor management complexity is determined by the patient's clinical picture during labor — which, for TOLAC patients, will always be complex.
Q15
Can a labor management code and delivery code be billed on the same day?
AMA Confirmed — Labor Management + Delivery Same Day
Yes. A delivery code may be reported on the same calendar date as an initial or subsequent labor management code, even when the same physician provides both services. This is one of the most common same-day coding scenarios under the new framework.
Example — Labor begins and delivery occurs same calendar day:
  • 59080 — Initial day, straightforward labor management
  • 59431 — Vaginal delivery (no prior cesarean)
  • 59433 — Third-degree laceration repair (if applicable, separately reportable)
Important: Once labor management begins, all other hospital care E/M codes stop. Exception: if the patient was seen in the office earlier that same day and then admitted for labor onset on the same calendar date, the office E/M may also be reported.

## PAGE 9 — INTRO TO INVESTING FOR TEENS

**Headline:** How to Make Your Money Make Money

**What is investing?**
Putting money into something that grows over time. The most beginner-friendly option: index funds.

**Index Fund = a basket of stocks**
Instead of picking one company, you own a tiny piece of hundreds of companies at once. If the market goes up, you go up.

**How to start (when you're ready):**
1. Ask a parent to help you open a custodial brokerage account (Fidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard)
2. Start with as little as $1
3. Buy an index fund (example: VTI or FXAIX)
4. Set up automatic monthly contributions
5. Don't touch it. Let it grow.

**The 3 rules of investing:**
- Start early (you just learned why)
- Be consistent (same amount every month)
- Stay calm (the market goes up AND down — don't panic sell)

**Terms to know:**
- **Stock:** Owning a tiny piece of a company
- **Index Fund:** A bundle of many stocks
- **Dividend:** A payment some companies make to investors
- **Portfolio:** All your investments together
- **Compound Interest:** Earnings on your earnings

**Design notes:** Glossary box in sidebar, numbered steps with icons, keep language completely jargon-free.
